# Combined AOI Analysis Summary (Voice Condition)

## Overview

This report combines two key analyses:
1. **First Non-Center Fixation** - Which AOI is fixated first (after ignoring center fixations)
2. **Total Fixations** - Overall fixation distribution across AOIs

**Note**: "other" AOI has been recategorized as "subject" in all analyses.

---

## 1. FIRST NON-CENTER FIXATION ANALYSIS

**Method**: Analyzed the first fixation that is >100 pixels from screen center (X=941, Y=499)

**Results**:
- **Object AOI**: 459 fixations (52.5% of trials)
- **Subject AOI**: 416 fixations (47.5% of trials)
- **Total trials analyzed**: 875

**Statistical Test**: Chi-square test (p = 0.146) - Not significantly different from 50/50 distribution

**Key Finding**: Slight numerical preference for object AOI as the first non-center fixation target, but not statistically significant.

### By Voice ID (First Non-Center Fixation):
- **ACT**: 50.9% object, 49.1% subject
- **PNA**: 53.3% object, 46.7% subject  
- **PWA**: 53.3% object, 46.7% subject

## 2. TOTAL FIXATIONS ANALYSIS

## COMPARISON: FIRST FIXATION vs TOTAL FIXATIONS

### Interesting Pattern:
1. **First Non-Center Fixation**: Object AOI slightly preferred (52.5% vs 47.5%)
2. **Total Fixations**: Subject AOI receives more fixations (53.0% vs 47.0%)

### Interpretation:

This pattern suggests:

1. **Initial Attention to Objects**: When first moving attention away from center, participants tend to fixate object AOI first (52.5% of trials). This could indicate:
   - Objects may be visually salient or positioned to attract initial attention
   - Voice guidance may initially direct attention to objects
   - Objects might be located in more peripheral/visible positions

2. **Sustained Attention to Subjects**: Despite initial object preference, subject AOI receives more total fixations (53% vs 47%). This suggests:
   - Once attention is directed, subjects receive more sustained viewing
   - Subjects may require more processing time (longer fixations or more revisits)
   - Subjects may be more informative or relevant for task completion

3. **Two-Stage Processing**: 
   - **Stage 1 (Initial)**: Object AOI attracts first non-center fixation
   - **Stage 2 (Sustained)**: Subject AOI receives more total viewing time

### Possible Explanations:

1. **Visual Layout**: Objects may be positioned to catch initial attention, while subjects require more sustained viewing
2. **Cognitive Processing**: Initial object fixation may guide subsequent attention to subjects
3. **Voice Guidance**: Voice may direct initial attention to objects, but subjects receive more processing overall
4. **Task Demands**: The task may require more attention to subjects once initial exploration is complete

---

## CONSISTENCY ACROSS VOICE TYPES

Both analyses show **consistent patterns across all three voice types** (ACT, PNA, PWA):

- Similar first fixation preferences (all ~50-53% object)
- Similar total fixation distributions (all ~52-54% subject)
- No major differences between voice types

This suggests the pattern is robust and not dependent on specific voice characteristics.
